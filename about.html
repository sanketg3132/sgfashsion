about us

The main objective of a fashion website is to keep the viewer in the site long enough for them to see the latest collection. Some fashion websites have interactive elements and try to transform the visit into a sensory experience for the user. In this post we have compiled 50 of the best fashion websites you can find on the Internet right now. Fashion is fickle, so their websites can vary from from one season to the next, so take a look now, before they're "so last season!"
Each fashion brand aims to create a unique and characteristic visual style that fits its personality. However, there are certain characteristics that are common to most fashion companies' websites. Most fashion websites rely on the power of visuals to draw attention and let the products speak for themselves. The use of photographs is imperative, hero images and videos are very popular. In most cases, the homepage consists of a large background photo and a navigation menu. The rest of the content is pushed to the background so it does not compete with the displayed products. Black and white is a common colour scheme for most fashion websites, especially those that want to convey an image of elegance and sophistication, while young and playful brands use a lot of colour.

Best Fashion Websites for Vintage and Indie Pieces

ASOS and Boohoo are great places to find items that your neighbors and friends are not likely to have. But you will primarily find trendy clothes, and if you aren’t looking for trendy, well, you may be disappointed.

Need Supply Co

For some of the nicest vintage clothes out there, check out the extremely popular Need Supply Co. Its store is based out of Richmond, VA, but it sells the majority of its clothes online. The brand started off by selling vintage Levi’s jeans and have since expanded their offering to include men’s and women’s clothes and accessories. Need Supply Co. curates a mix of both known brands and up-and-coming independent designers from America and around the world. Its clothes are definitely not cheap, with a t-shirt costing anywhere from $70 and up, to jumpsuits costing an average of $200. The company ships worldwide, offers a 10% discount on your first order, and has an interesting editorial section with articles and thought-leadership pieces from designers.
